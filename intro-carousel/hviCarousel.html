1.5 million New York City residents are vulnerable to increased heat caused by climate change.

The NYC Department of Health and Mental Hygiene has created a Heat Vulnerability Index to identify the neighborhoods most vulnerable to heat waves.

Heat Vulnerability is not equally distributed across New York City boroughs.

For example, Bedford Stuyvesant in Brooklyn has a much higher Heat Vulnerability Indexthan Manhattan's Upper West Side.

During summer 2020, NYC launched the "Cool Streets" program to provide outdoor cooling options in the most heat-burdened parts of the city.
