The COVID-19 crisis has quieted the buzzing street life of New York City.

We used location data from February and March 2020 to detect changes in street activity during the early phases of the COVID-19 pandemic in NYC.

In March 2020, COVID-19 turned Midtown into a ghost town.

In Times Square, the heart of New York's real estate and tourism economy, street activity decreased 99% during weekday morning rush hours.

However, other boroughs reported an increase in activity on local neighborhood streets, especially on weekends.

Sadly, upticks in street activity were detected around hospitals, which have been on the frontlines of the coronavirus pandemic.
